Report — executive summary, roadmap, quick wins, not-recommended

1 · Executive summary

The game is in the best shape it has ever been: the De Stijl system holds across almost every surface, the guess mechanic gives rounds real endings, the decks speak with one (occasionally too-recognisable) voice, and the 373MB→44MB asset work makes it deployable. This pass found no P0s and three P1s — one of which quietly kills the entire first-run experience: the manifesto and onboarding tips never actually fire for a genuinely new player (BUG2-01). The other two P1s: the light-theme round reveal renders its names white-on-white (BUG2-02), and the base question deck — the one every player sees most — is the thinnest deck in the game at 39 prompts (DECK2-01). Beyond those, the findings cluster into four themes: (a) the flavour-pool repetition class (same joke twice on one board) that v1 fixed for Judgement but not the other modes; (b) a first-five-minutes funnel that buries the premise and over-fronts settings; (c) a writing tic inventory measured across all 1,397 prompts (11.6% end on the same 1–3-word punch); and (d) online polish — the question card is not actually synchronised between devices, and host-takeover authority doesn't survive a refresh.

18 · Ideas considered but NOT recommended

• Audience/companion voting app — a second screen fractures a pass-and-play party game; the DOUBT micro-vote (FUN2-03) gets 90% of the value with zero infrastructure. • Accounts / cloud saves — against the local-first design; the save-key + seed links already cover continuity. • Default-on round timers — timers fight social pacing; ship as an opt-in modifier only (FUN2-01). • Moderation/reporting systems — meaningless for a local party game with no UGC distribution. • Full ASCII mode now — already queued on the roadmap in modes.js; do it as content, not as part of this audit. • Rewriting all 1,397 prompts — the voice is fundamentally right; targeted de-ticking beats a rewrite.
